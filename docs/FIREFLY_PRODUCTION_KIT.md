# Sons of Disparity — Firefly Still Production Kit

The film is being rebuilt as **animated stills**: 5–7 drawings per scene, moved by
programmatic slow pans and crossfades (no AI video animation — that's where every
style/character failure came from). This kit is everything needed to generate the
stills in Adobe Firefly. It is written to be followed by a human or by Claude in
Chrome operating firefly.adobe.com.

## The character board

A Firefly Board named **"Sons of Disparity — Deon Character & Sketchbook Style"**
is already in this Adobe account (Firefly → Files → Boards). It holds 10 approved
references:

- **Character refs (3):** the plea table (Deon left, natural-hair silhouette),
  the count (Deon reading on the bunk), the rearview mirror (Deon from behind).
- **Style refs (7):** ledger pages, night courtyard with laundry, locker +
  backpack, plea paper under the lamp, empty cell with window beam, the silent
  telephone, the seedling windowsill.

Use one of these as a **Style Reference** on *every* generation. When a new shot
is close to an existing composition, also use it as a Structure Reference.

## The character sheet — read before every figure shot

**DEON** is rendered ONLY as a completely solid black ink silhouette — a shadow
puppet. Never any facial features, eyes, nose, mouth, or skin. His identity lives
entirely in his outline, which must stay constant:

- **Hair: short, tightly coiled natural hair — the afro texture must be readable
  in the silhouette outline.** This is the single most important consistency cue
  and the one that failed before. If the outline could read as straight or
  Caucasian-coded hair, reject the image.
- Build: lean. Ages: ~12 (small, backpack), 19 (lean young man), mid-20s
  (father, slightly broader shoulders).

**MOTHER / GRANDMOTHER:** solid silhouettes, natural hair readable in outline.
**MARCUS:** infant, solid silhouette in a blanket.
**TYLER** (plea scene only): solid silhouette with straight, flat combed hair —
the outline contrast against Deon is the point.

## Master style prompt (prepend to every generation)

> Hand-drawn pen and ink illustration on aged cream sketchbook paper with worn
> edges, vintage archival documentary style, crosshatched pen shading. Human
> figures rendered as completely solid black ink silhouettes, filled in entirely
> like shadow puppets — no facial features, no eyes, no nose, no mouth, no skin
> detail anywhere. Quiet, dignified, humane. No photorealism, no bright colors,
> no readable words or lettering.

**Color rules:** pure B&W ink on cream everywhere, with exactly two exceptions —
a **faint red wash** only in the-stop scenes, and **muted watercolor green** on
the seedling only in the-nursery scenes.
**Format:** 16:9 landscape, largest available size. Generate 4 variants per
shot and keep the best 1–2.

## Rejection checklist (apply to every candidate)

Reject if: any facial feature or skin visible · Deon's hair outline not clearly
natural/coiled · props era-broken (the car must be an ordinary **modern** sedan,
2000s+, not vintage) · style drifts photo-real or comic · any readable text ·
color outside the two exceptions · figure present in a shot listed as empty.

## Shot lists (5–7 per scene, 16:9)

### 1 · the-ledger (prologue)
1. A closed ledger book on a desk in lamplight, wide
2. The ledger lying open, first pages, pen in the gutter
3. Overhead close view of columns of illegible cursive entries *(match board ref)*
4. Extreme close: a pen nib underlining one illegible entry
5. The ledger's page edge dissolving into a sketched city skyline at night
6. The city skyline alone, streetlamps, night, wide

### 2 · before-he-has-a-name
1. A young Black mother (natural-hair silhouette) holding her infant beside a tenement window
2. The same window seen from outside at night — the pair as silhouettes in one lit window
3. The block below: courtyard, laundry lines, streetlamp *(match board ref)*
4. Extreme close: the infant's silhouette hand curled around the mother's finger
5. A grandmother silhouette in a doorway, watching, tender
6. The street seen through glass at a child's eye height

### 3 · the-hallway
1. A long school hallway, a small Black boy (backpack, natural-hair silhouette) walking away alone
2. One locker ajar, backpack on the floor *(match board ref)*
3. Two small silhouettes mid-shove seen far away through a door's window — ambiguous, no violence detail
4. A frosted-glass office door, a small silhouette waiting on a bench outside
5. Close: a discipline form and pen on a desk, all writing illegible
6. Mother and boy silhouettes walking out the school's front doors into flat daylight

### 4 · the-stop  *(faint red wash allowed; car MUST be a modern ordinary sedan)*
1. A modern sedan parked at the night curb under one streetlamp, single driver silhouette (natural hair), faint red wash at the frame's rear edge
2. Interior from the back seat: rearview mirror haloed in red glow, driver silhouette alone *(match board ref)*
3. Close: a printed résumé on the empty passenger seat, text illegible
4. Through the side window: the driver's silhouette, hands resting on the wheel
5. The car's shadow stretched long across the asphalt by headlights from behind
6. Wide from across the street: the small car in two cones of light

### 5 · the-plea
1. Two silhouettes across a bare table under a hanging lamp — Deon left (coiled hair), Tyler right (straight flat hair) *(match board ref)*
2. Close on Deon's side: folded silhouette hands on the table
3. A wall clock with smudged, unreadable hands
4. The plea paper and pen alone under the lamp, empty chair pushed back *(match board ref)*
5. Tyler's side: the door of light standing open behind his silhouette
6. Two sketched doors side by side: one open and bright, one shut and crosshatched dark
7. Extreme close: a silhouette hand signing, marks illegible

### 6 · the-count
1. The cell block wide: Deon's silhouette reading on a bunk by a small lamp *(match board ref)*
2. The empty cell: barred window, beam of light on the floor *(match board ref)*
3. Close: an open book in solid silhouette hands
4. A row of identical cell doors receding into crosshatch
5. A half-written letter home and a pencil stub, writing illegible
6. Tally marks scratched on a wall, unreadable, many

### 7 · the-box
1. Kitchen table, front-on: a Black silhouette hand poised with a pen, the form page edge-on and unreadable
2. The silent telephone in window light *(match board ref)*
3. The form blurred except one heavy checkbox, all text illegible
4. Deon's silhouette at the window, waiting, a cup going cold
5. A bus-stop bench at dawn, one silhouette seated, streetlight still on
6. The telephone again at night, still silent, moonlit

### 8 · the-cross-examination  *(the bookend — mostly objects, no new figures needed)*
1. The ledger reopened on a bare table under a single lamp
2. A hand-drawn scale-of-justice diagram, visibly uneven, ink on cream
3. One sketched road forking into two diverging roads
4. A hand-drawn chart motif: two lines converging for decades, then splitting apart — no readable labels
5. Shelves of identical file folders receding
6. An empty sketched mirror frame facing the reader
7. A pen at rest beside the ledger, question left open

### 9 · the-nursery  *(muted green on the seedling ONLY — and the father MUST read Black)*
1. A Black father — **short coiled natural hair unmistakable in the silhouette outline** — holding his infant beside a tall window at dawn, the green seedling on the sill
2. The seedling close on the windowsill beside a folded blanket *(match board ref)*
3. The father's silhouette hand under the infant's tiny hand
4. Crib and rocking chair in soft dawn light, empty, waiting
5. The window from outside: father and child silhouettes in the one lit window at dawn
6. The seedling with one new leaf, morning light strengthening

## Export & handoff

- Download keepers as PNG, largest size.
- Name them `<scene-slug>-01.png` … `<scene-slug>-07.png` using the slugs above
  (e.g. `the-stop-03.png`).
- Bring them back to the Claude Code session any way that's convenient: a zip
  upload, a Firefly share link, or public URLs.

## What happens on return (already prepared)

Claude Code will: screen every image against the rejection checklist once more →
apply a uniform finishing pass (tone/grain) so all batches read as one sketchbook
→ compress → build the multi-still Ken Burns engine (slow pans + crossfades, 5–7
stills per scene) → rewire the story acts and cinema player → deploy to
sonsofdisparity.com and re-migrate the database. Narration, timing, overlays, and
captions all carry over unchanged.
